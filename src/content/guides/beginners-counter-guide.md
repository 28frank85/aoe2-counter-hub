If you're new to Age of Empires 2, losing feels random — the opponent's army deleted yours and you
don't know why. The answer is almost always **counters**. This guide explains the system from zero,
so you can walk into any game knowing what to build and why.

## Why Units Lose — The Rock-Paper-Scissors Principle

Age of Empires 2 does not have a single "best unit." Every powerful unit has a designated weakness, and every
cheap unit counters something expensive. The game is designed as layered rock-paper-scissors:

| Unit Type | Beats | Loses To |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cavalry (Knights) | Archers, Siege | Spearmen, Camels |
| Archers | Infantry (at range) | Skirmishers, Mangonels |
| Skirmishers | Archers | Melee Infantry (Champions) |
| Spearmen / Halbs | Cavalry | Archers, Siege |
| Siege (Trebuchet) | Buildings, Massed units | Cavalry (Hussars) |
| Monks | Expensive units (conversion) | Cavalry charge, Hussar raid |

This is why experienced players constantly scout — the moment they see what the opponent is making,
they switch to the counter. **The counter, not the stronger army, wins most games.**

## Hard Counter vs. Soft Counter — What's the Difference?

This guide (and the interactive app) labels every counter as either **hard** or **soft**. Here is
what that means:

- **Hard counter** — has a direct attack bonus vs. the target's armor class, or a specific armor type
  that resists the target's damage. Wins the fight efficiently in *equal numbers*. Example:
  Halberdier vs. Knight — the Halb has +32 attack bonus and wins 1v1.
- **Soft counter** — wins through speed, positioning, or raw stats — but needs a cost advantage,
  number advantage, or micro to be reliable. Example: Hussar vs. Arbalest — the Hussar needs to close
  the distance and can take a lot of arrows on the way in. It works but it's not clean.

> **Rule of thumb:** If you're in a desperate situation, use your hard counter. If you're ahead and
> have options, soft counters can be more efficient. In equal games, always go for the hard counter
> first.

## The 5 Rules of Countering

### 1. Scout first

You cannot counter what you don't see. Use your Scout (or Eagle Scout for Meso civs) to explore the
opponent's base early. See Stables? Expect cavalry. See Archery Ranges? Expect archers. See a Castle
being placed? A unique unit is coming. Check the opponent's production buildings every 2–3 minutes and
adjust your own production.

### 2. Build the counter, not the mirror

If your opponent is making Knights, do not make Knights back — unless you have a civ-specific reason.
Make Halberdiers or Camels instead. They cost less and beat Knights cleanly. Matching the opponent's
unit choice only works if your version is significantly better (better civ bonuses, more upgrades).

### 3. Produce before you need it

By the time you see 10 Knights attacking your base, 2 Halberdiers won't save you. Start counter
production the moment you see the first threat unit. Production rate matters as much as the counter
itself. Queue the counter units before the army arrives, not during the attack.

### 4. Hard counters work in equal numbers. Soft counters need advantages.

A Halberdier beats a Knight 1v1 — that is a hard counter. A Hussar does not beat a Knight 1v1 (Hussars
are soft counters to cavalry archers, not Knights directly). Know which category your counter falls
into and scale your production accordingly.

### 5. Siege is never the counter to siege

Don't try to out-siege the opponent's siege weapons with your own. Fight siege with cavalry.
Trebuchets and Mangonels die instantly to Hussars that reach them. Two Hussars kill a Trebuchet. Save
your Siege for fighting armies and buildings — use cavalry to eliminate the opponent's siege.

## How to Scout and Identify Threats

Scouting is the skill that unlocks the counter system. Here is what to look for at each age:

### Dark Age (starting)

- Find the opponent's base location — note the map layout
- Check how many sheep they have (economic info)
- Watch for Boar luring near their town center — measures their eco efficiency

### Feudal Age

- **Barracks only** → Man-at-Arms rush or Militia → make Archers or Spearmen
- **Archery Range** → Archer rush → make Skirmishers immediately
- **Stable** → Scout rush → make Spearmen immediately
- **Blacksmith active** → watch which techs they research (Attack = aggression, Armor = defensive push)

### Castle Age

- **Multiple Stables** → Knight rush coming → get Halbs/Camels up before first Knights arrive
- **Multiple Archery Ranges** → Crossbowman/Arbalest push → upgrade Skirms to Elite, add Mangonel
- **Castle placed forward** → unique unit is coming → check the civ and look up their UU counter
- **Siege Workshop** → Mangonels or Rams → keep cavalry near your base as raid option

## The 5 Units Every Beginner Must Know

These five units form the backbone of countering. Learn to produce them instantly when needed — they
cover the most common threats at every stage.

| Unit | What it counters | When to make it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Elite Skirmisher | All archers (Archer, Crossbowman, Arbalest, Hand Cannoneer) | The moment you see an Archery Range — upgrade to Elite ASAP |
| Halberdier | All cavalry (Scout → Paladin) | When opponent builds Stables — upgrade before Knights arrive |
| Mangonel | Grouped infantry, grouped archers, Skirmishers | When the opponent has massed units in formation |
| Monk | Expensive units — Knights, Paladins, unique units, siege | When the opponent commits gold-heavy units; always keep 3–5 Monks |
| Hussar | All siege weapons + economic raids | When the opponent has Trebuchets, Mangonels, or Bombard Cannons |

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | What to do instead |
| --- | --- |
| Making Spearmen vs. Archers | Make Skirmishers — Spearmen have low pierce armor and die to arrows |
| Making Halbs vs. everything | Halbs only counter cavalry — they die to archers and siege |
| Ignoring Monks | A Monk converting a Paladin (320 resources) pays for 3 Monks instantly |
| Building one unit type only | Mix: always add Siege to support your army + a mobile unit to protect Siege |
| Not scouting after Castle Age | Scout every 2–3 minutes — the opponent's composition changes as they tech up |
| Fighting siege with infantry | Use cavalry — Hussars kill Trebuchets in 3 hits; infantry takes 20+ |
